# DevMatrix

## The idea in one line
**DevMatrix is a multiplayer space-combat game where players don’t pilot ships directly — they command them in natural language, and their AI captains execute those tactics in real time.**

## Hackathon pitch
Most competitive games reward fast hands. DevMatrix rewards fast thinking.

In DevMatrix, every player controls an autonomous combat ship by writing short tactical prompts like:
- “Orbit the nearest enemy and burst fire.”
- “Flank from behind and defend rear shields.”
- “If health is low, disengage and evade.”

Your ship keeps acting on your strategy between commands, so battles become a mind game of planning, adaptation, and counter-play — not button mashing.

## Core player experience
- **Live shared battlefield:** everyone fights in the same persistent arena.
- **Prompt-to-action combat:** natural language orders become active combat behavior.
- **Strategy over reflexes:** success comes from better tactical intent and timing.
- **Readable combat feedback:** players can see behavior, kills, deaths, and leaderboard movement as fights unfold.

## Progression loop
DevMatrix combines AI command gameplay with long-term progression:
1. Enter matches and command your ship.
2. Earn rewards through combat outcomes.
3. Upgrade hulls, weapons, shields, and AI processors.
4. Unlock deeper tactical expression as your AI tier improves.
5. Return stronger and smarter for the next battle.

This creates a compelling loop where progression improves both **power** and **decision depth**.

## Why this is interesting
- **New control paradigm:** “prompt-driven PvP” is a fresh genre blend.
- **High replayability:** different tactics and counters create emergent battles.
- **Built for spectatorship:** behavior-driven fights are easy to follow and fun to watch.
- **Strong expansion potential:** tournaments, squad roles, ranked seasons, and creator-defined tactics can all build on the same concept.

## Vision
DevMatrix aims to become a competitive sandbox where human creativity and AI execution meet — a game where the best commander wins.
